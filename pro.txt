    <h2>What is Air Quality Index?</h2>
    <p>The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a numerical score that reflects the level of air pollution in a given location.<br>
       It helps individuals understand the health risks associated with breathing the air 
       <br>and provides guidance on activities they can take to protect themselves.</p>

    <h2>Understanding AQI Levels in India</h2>
    <p>The Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) of India categorizes AQI levels with corresponding health impacts as follows:<br>
      <a target=# href="https://airquality.cpcb.gov.in/ccr/">Click Here to Know more</a></p>
    <ul class="Index">
      <li><b>Good (0-50):</b> Minimal impact on public health.</li>
      <li><b>Moderate (51-100):</b> May cause breathing discomfort to sensitive individuals, particularly those with lung issues.</li>
      <li><b>Poor (101-200):</b> May cause breathing problems, coughing, and irritation of the eyes, nose, and throat in everyone.</li>
      <li><b>Very Poor (201-300):</b> May cause respiratory illness to people with heart or lung disease. Healthy people may experience reduced tolerance to physical activity.</li>
      <li><b>Severe (301-400):</b> May cause serious health problems to everyone. Limit outdoor activity.</li>
      <li><b>Severe+ (401+):</b> Emergency conditions. Healthy people experience respiratory illness. Everyone should avoid outdoor activity.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2>Causes of Air Pollution</h2>
    <ul id="pollution-causes">
      <li>Vehicle Emissions: Cars, trucks, and buses release harmful pollutants like nitrogen oxides and particulate matter.</li>
      <li>Industrial Activities: Factories and power plants emit pollutants like sulfur dioxide and volatile organic compounds.</li>
      <li>Burning Fossil Fuels: The burning of coal, oil, and natural gas releases pollutants into the atmosphere.</li>
      <li>Agricultural Practices: Burning of agricultural waste and dust from tilling contribute to air pollution.</li>
      <li>Natural Sources: Wildfires, volcanic eruptions, and dust storms can also impact air quality.</li>
    </ul>
    <h2>Improving Air Quality</h2>
    <ul id="pollution-solutions">
      <li>Use Public Transport or Cycle: Reduce reliance on private vehicles.</li>
      <li>Conserve Energy: Reduce energy consumption at home and work to decrease emissions from power plants.</li>
      <li>Support Renewable Energy: Advocate for policies that encourage solar, wind, and other renewable energy sources.</li>
      <li>Reduce, Reuse, Recycle: Minimize waste generation to lessen pollution from waste incineration.</li>
      <li>Plant Trees: Trees act as natural air filters, absorbing pollutants.</li>
    </ul>

const aqiDataElement = document.getElementById('aqi-data');
const getAqiButton = document.getElementById('get-aqi');

getAqiButton.addEventListener('click', getAqiData);

function getAqiData() {
  aqiDataElement.textContent = 'Fetching data...';

  // Replace 'YOUR_API_KEY' with your actual API key
  const apiKey = 'YOUR_API_KEY';
  const location = { lat: 37.7749, lon: -122.4194 }; // Example coordinates (San Francisco)
  
  // You'll need to choose an Air Quality API and update the URL and logic accordingly
  const url = `https://api.example.com/air-quality?lat=${location.lat}&lon=${location.lon}&key=${apiKey}`;

  fetch(url)
    .then(response => response.json())
    .then(data => {
      const aqi = data.aqi;
      aqiDataElement.textContent = `AQI: ${aqi}`;
    })
    .catch(error => {
      console.error('Error fetching AQI data:', error);
      aqiDataElement.textContent = 'Error fetching data.';
    });
}
